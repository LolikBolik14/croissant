What is a Croissant?

A croissant is a flaky yeast baked product containing layers of dough/coagulated fat to create a flaky crunchy texture. Croissants are categorized as Viennoiserie baked goods or confectionery along with buns, Danish and puff pastries. A croissant usually contains normal amounts of salt, yeast, and sugar.1 The texture and flavor of croissants are determined by:

  1. Lamination: a folding process creates alternating dough and fat layers
  2. Expansion: caused by the leavening action of yeast fermentation, and physical separation of the dough and fat layers during the oven spring stage of baking as the water trapped between them vaporizes to steam.
